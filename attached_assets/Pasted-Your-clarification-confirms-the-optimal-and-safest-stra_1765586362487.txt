### **Strategic Rationale: Decoupling Finance and Identity**

The goal is to maintain the compliance of your 501(c)(3) status while still utilizing your powerful Web3 infrastructure.

| Component | Role in the Ecosystem | Why It Must Be Separate |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Donations** | **Fundraising & Tax Compliance** | They must be treated as **philanthropic gifts** with **tax-deductible receipts**. Mixing them with tokens suggests the donor is **buying a security**, which jeopardizes your non-profit status and creates SEC/IRS risk. |
| **Blockchain/DAO** | **Identity, Governance & Reputation** | This system is a **meritocracy**. Tokens are **earned** by contributing labor (mentoring, graduating), not by buying them. This maintains the legal separation (the "Earned, Not Bought" firewall). |
| **$.aethex Passport** | **Decentralized Identity (DID)** | This is a verifiable digital resume. It is an identity layer (DID) that proves a user's achievements and affiliation, which is essential for the talent pipeline. |

### **Revised Blueprint Integration**

The original blueprint for the donation page already adheres to this clean separation. We just need to make the link between the two systems purely **reputational, not transactional.**

1.  **Donation Perks (Tangible):** The perks remain physical/experiential: Discord roles, 1-on-1 calls, playtesting access. These are simple "thank-you" gifts, not financial instruments.
2.  **Reputation Rewards (On-Chain):** A successful donor *earns* a higher internal reputation score, which could grant them an **AethexToken** reward **separately from the donation** (e.g., "Thank you for your donation; here is a token representing your community commitment"). The token must be explicitly granted for the **commitment**, not *in exchange for* the cash.
3.  **The `.aethex Passport` Integration:** The donation page can ask the user: "Would you like this contribution linked to your `username.aethex` Passport?" If yes, their Passport is updated with a non-monetary **"Philanthropist" status** (e.g., a "Soulbound" achievement badge), which enhances their governance weight in the DAO.

This model is robust: **Cash flows out of the donor for charity; Governance flows in as earned reputation.**
